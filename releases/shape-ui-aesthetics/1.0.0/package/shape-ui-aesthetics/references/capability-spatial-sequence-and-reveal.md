# Spatial Sequence & Reveal

**Stable ID:** `spatial-sequence-and-reveal`  
**Class:** Core  
**Primary domain:** Spatial Composition

## Observable trigger

Route here when meaning depends on approach, passage through stages, concealment, threshold, pause, or reveal, and the current sequence spends its climax too early or turns navigation into undifferentiated traversal.

## Non-trigger and near miss

Do not route here when the destination and sequence are already legible and the problem is static mass hierarchy. If a simple path only needs clearer labels or action scent, return `reframe` and signal Interaction, Agency & Wayfinding. A critical emergency path must not be dramatized merely because sequence could feel richer.

## Decision question

What should users perceive, expect, and do at each stage from approach through arrival, and how should compression, deflection, preview, concealment, pause, and release serve that promise?

## Interpretation method

Treat circulation as temporal composition. Define why the destination is worth reaching, then map approach, entry recognition, threshold crossing, path relations, pauses, focal turns, and arrival from continuous user-height viewpoints. Coordinate visible evidence, movement, and changing expectation. Let the destination strengthen the path and the path prepare the destination.

## Executable design procedure

1. Name the destination, user promise, directness obligation, and completion state.
2. Draw the experiential timeline from first approach through arrival and recovery.
3. Record what is visible, actionable, and expected at each consequential viewpoint.
4. Assign direct, deflected, encircling, compressed, expanded, previewed, concealed, paused, or released relations only where they serve meaning or orientation.
5. Place thresholds and focal turns; preserve a clear route and escape from each.
6. Walk the sequence at realistic speed, density, viewport, input, and interruption conditions.
7. Remove meaningless bends, premature climaxes, and repeated reveals.

## Boundaries and failure modes

- Prioritize directness, accessibility, evacuation, fatigue control, and safety in hospitals, transport, emergency, and high-pressure paths.
- Do not create blind spots, collision risks, inaccessible thresholds, or orientation loss for drama.
- Do not assume rituals or narrative readings transfer across cultures or users.
- Validate density, speed, weather, maintenance, and actual use rather than empty ideal views.
- Reframe when the focal endpoint has no real product action or value.

## Attached Reference Knowledge

Sequence is a score of expectation and bodily movement, not a line between screens. Preview can sustain orientation; concealment can preserve anticipation; compression makes release perceptible. Each technique earns its place only when it improves the product ritual rather than delaying it.

## Runtime Self-Critique

Ask whether each stage changes expectation or agency, whether the climax is earned, whether a user can recover or take a direct path, and whether the sequence remains meaningful at realistic speed and density.
