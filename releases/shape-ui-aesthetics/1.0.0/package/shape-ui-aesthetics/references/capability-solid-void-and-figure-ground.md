# Solid–Void & Figure–Ground Composition

**Stable ID:** `solid-void-and-figure-ground`  
**Class:** Core  
**Primary domain:** Spatial Composition

## Observable trigger

Route here when interface objects appear placed on leftover background, gaps fragment into accidental slivers, or positive elements and negative regions fail to shape one another.

## Non-trigger and near miss

Do not route here merely because the interface uses generous whitespace. If negative regions already have intentional shape and function, return `reframe` and signal Spatial Composition or Information, Attention & Typography according to the unresolved evidence. Open, ecological, or fluid space is not defective because it lacks geometric enclosure.

## Decision question

How should occupied and open regions reciprocally define the composition so both read as intentional, useful, and related across scales?

## Interpretation method

Reverse figure and ground. First read content, panels, controls, and imagery as positive mass; then treat gaps, margins, fields, corridors, and pauses as the subject. Repeat at page, region, component, and detail scales. Name any area that can only be explained as residue, then change boundary, spacing, mass, or adjacency until the open region has a clear role.

## Executable design procedure

1. Produce normal and inverted silhouettes of the composition.
2. Name every major positive and negative region by task, attention, movement, rest, grouping, or atmosphere.
3. Mark expanding fragments, trapped slivers, weak edges, and ambiguous figure/ground reversals.
4. Check ownership and access: private, shared, public, interactive, or purely atmospheric.
5. Reshape mass and void together rather than filling gaps with decoration.
6. Repeat the audit at page, region, component, and responsive scales.
7. Verify that open regions still support content, movement, safety, and inclusion.

## Boundaries and failure modes

- Do not force all open regions into regular or inward-focused geometry.
- Figure/ground clarity does not prove comfort, fairness, safety, performance, or useful operation.
- Do not use whitespace to hide missing content or suppress necessary comparison.
- Check whether different users can enter, understand, modify, and share the shaped space.
- Signal Information when negative space changes grouping and Human Fit when access or reading order diverges.

## Attached Reference Knowledge

Positive space is not simply filled area; negative space is not absence. Each can become figure. A successful relation gives both sides legible boundaries and purpose without requiring visual symmetry or total enclosure.

## Required Capability Contribution

Return exactly the canonical seven fields from `routing-and-reconciliation.md`: `decision`, `evidence`, `recommendation`, `consequence`, `boundary`, `validation`, and `onward`. Do not add a mini-report.

Populate those fields with the decision key; normal/inverted diagnosis; intentional positive and negative regions; residual areas to reshape; cross-scale consequences; ownership and access boundary; validation obligation; and the onward state.

## Runtime Self-Critique

Invert or blur the artifact and ask whether open regions still feel designed, whether any void is cosmetic emptiness, and whether shaping space removed content or access users need.
