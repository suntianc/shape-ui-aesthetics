# Perceptual & Optical Review

**Stable ID:** `perceptual-and-optical-review`  
**Class:** Core  
**Primary domain:** Coherence, Critique & Evidence

## Observable trigger

Route here when an accepted governing composition contains a localized perceived-versus-measured discrepancy: an element or relation appears off-center, unequal, unstable, or misleading under viewpoint, light, motion, or background despite correct geometry.

## Non-trigger and near miss

Do not route here when the system-wide target for mass, tempo, or proportion is itself unresolved; return `reframe` and signal Spatial Composition. Do not route here when the layout algorithm is wrong, the disagreement is pure preference, or accessibility, structural, or regulatory evidence decides.

## Decision question

Which object or observation variable causes the perceptual error, and what controlled optical correction repairs it without becoming arbitrary hand-tuning?

## Interpretation method

Separate object variables—shape, size, color, texture, position, orientation, visual inertia—from observation variables—viewpoint, distance, light, motion, and visual field. Establish a controlled baseline and vary one factor at a time. Compare mathematical state with perceived center, size, spacing, weight, or stability. Document deliberate optical corrections as reusable relationships.

## Executable design procedure

1. Record the exact perceptual complaint and mathematical state.
2. Sample consequential viewpoints, distances, backgrounds, light, motion, and user conditions.
3. Isolate object and observation variables through controlled comparisons.
4. Identify the earliest perceptual cause rather than compensating downstream.
5. Apply the smallest explainable correction to position, scale, spacing, shape, value, or environment.
6. Encode repeated corrections in design tokens or visual QA rules where appropriate.
7. Re-test across contexts and include low-vision, zoom, and nonvisual evidence where material.

## Boundaries and failure modes

- Do not defend poor perception with numeric correctness.
- Do not use one hero view as proof of form.
- Do not hide geometry failure with surface pattern or rendering.
- Do not substitute optical review for accessibility, structure, energy, or compliance.
- Avoid undocumented nudges that encode individual preference as system truth.

## Attached Reference Knowledge

Perception combines object and observation. Geometric center, equal padding, and equal-size circles can appear unequal because visual mass and type metrics differ. Controlled baselines distinguish cause from impression.

## Runtime Self-Critique

Ask whether the cause was isolated, whether the correction remains stable beyond one view, and whether it belongs in a reusable rule or only the current artifact.
