# Spatial Continuity & Orientation

**Stable ID:** `spatial-continuity-and-orientation`  
**Class:** Core  
**Primary domain:** Motion, Rhythm & Causality

## Observable trigger

Route here when a transition severs origin, destination, object identity, reversibility, layer relation, or the user's sense of where content can be found afterward.

## Non-trigger and near miss

Do not route here when static change is already clearest, when the issue is CTA salience, request acknowledgement, or isolated timing after the spatial model is proven.

## Decision question

What stable spatial and object relationship should a transition teach, and how can users understand the same change without relying on motion?

## Interpretation method

Map before and after states, shared objects, trigger, origin, destination, storage location, and reverse path. Choose one consistent spatial metaphor expressed through direction, path, scale, morph, persistence, or direct manipulation. Use motion only to lower uncertainty. Temporarily quiet competition without making nonfocused content undiscoverable or unusable.

## Executable design procedure

1. Diagram the state delta and where each object comes from, goes, remains, and returns.
2. Identify persistent elements and the trigger point that can anchor continuity.
3. Choose one spatial metaphor and apply direction and depth consistently.
4. Keep related content traceable; avoid whole-screen replacement when a local transformation explains more.
5. Preserve entry, close, undo, focus, and retrieval paths for pointer, touch, keyboard, and assistive use.
6. Create a no-motion equivalent with explicit state and location cues.
7. Test whether users can recount the change and find the object again.

## Boundaries and failure modes

- Do not animate when it delays the task, causes discomfort, or hides information.
- Avoid motion originating from arbitrary directions or teaching inconsistent spatial rules.
- Do not make hover the only route to isolation or retrieval.
- Preserve direct manipulation only when results are reversible and recoverable.
- Signal Human Fit for expectation and accessibility, and Medium for spatial-computing-specific models.

## Attached Reference Knowledge

Screens imply space beyond the viewport. Persistent elements, origin-aware expansion, and mirrored return paths make that space learnable. Continuity is a mental-model obligation, not a polish layer.
