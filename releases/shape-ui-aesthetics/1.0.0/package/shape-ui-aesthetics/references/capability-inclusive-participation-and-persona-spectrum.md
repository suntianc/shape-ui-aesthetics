# Inclusive Participation & Persona Spectrum

**Stable ID:** `inclusive-participation-and-persona-spectrum`  
**Class:** Core  
**Primary domain:** Human Fit, Cognition & Inclusion

## Observable trigger

Route here when one assumed user, body, ability, role, pace, input, language, or independent-completion path excludes people from the same core activity or shared outcome.

## Non-trigger and near miss

Do not route here for one known technical accessibility defect, demographic segmentation, legal compliance judgment, or a single preference without observable task mismatch.

## Decision question

Which shared motivation and outcome should unite different participation paths, and how can affected people change the solution rather than merely receive adaptations?

## Interpretation method

Separate accessibility properties, inclusive design process, and broadly usable outcome. Map mismatch within a specific task across permanent, temporary, and situational conditions. Start with a person experiencing a consequential mismatch, identify the shared motivation, solve with them, then test adjacent conditions without assuming automatic transfer. Allow interdependent contributions among people, tools, assistants, and groups; independence is not the only dignified success model.

## Executable design procedure

1. Define the core activity, shared outcome, and who currently cannot enter, contribute, collaborate, or receive feedback.
2. Map perception, action, language, tool, role, pace, environment, and support mismatches.
3. Co-design with affected people and distinguish access attributes from process and outcome claims.
4. Generate complementary participation paths that preserve the same core activity and result.
5. Check where effort, care, cost, and maintenance shift, especially onto lower-power participants.
6. Test adjacent spectrum conditions for function, burden, dignity, identity, and belonging.
7. Record what remains a legal, governance, resource, or cultural question.

## Boundaries and failure modes

- Do not equate standards compliance with inclusive process or emotional belonging.
- Do not seek one lowest-common-denominator solution for everyone.
- Do not use averages or demographic labels as substitutes for task evidence.
- Do not claim one person's solution automatically generalizes.
- Escalate rights, representation, structural resource, and governance decisions to accountable processes.

## Attached Reference Knowledge

Accessibility is an experience property; inclusive design changes who shapes the work; universal design seeks broad usability. Persona spectra map task mismatch, not human worth. Multi-path participation can be interdependent while remaining equitable and dignified.

## Runtime Self-Critique

Ask who defined normal use, whose labor the alternative requires, whether all paths reach the same meaningful outcome, and whether affected people had authority to alter the direction.
