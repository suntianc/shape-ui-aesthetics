Diagnosis / interaction risks

Consequential weaknesses requiring correction

Ranked by severity. High = breaks the training model. Medium = degrades trust or comprehension.

High Obstruction injection changes position without earned travel

Invoking "Trainer: close Aisle 3" after planning immediately records 46% even if the guided run had not advanced there. That breaks the causal link between last perceived position and reported position. The trainee sees the vehicle jump to an unearned coordinate.

cause: state.progress = Math.max(state.progress, .46) — clamps progress to minimum 46% on failure injection regardless of actual travel
High Shared percentage applied to different route geometries

Recovery retains 46% while replacing the direct path with the Aisle 6 detour. Equal percentages on paths with different segments do not guarantee the same world coordinate, so the vehicle can jump when the alternate route appears. Recovery must anchor to the last confirmed world position, then build a route from that point.

cause: state.progress is not recomputed relative to the detour route; interpolateRoute uses the same t value on a different geometry
Medium Free camera lacks task viewpoints

The camera reports degrees and can reset, but it has no named origin, blind-corner approach, operator eye level, or destination views. A trainee can inspect the survey model yet cannot compare that survey to a constrained travel perspective.

cause: no named camera bookmark system — only yaw/pitch/zoom state and reset to initial values
Medium Recovery is system-selected, not trainer-authored

"Reroute via Aisle 6" is clear, but the fixture does not expose why that path is viable, who authorized it, or whether stopping and asking a floor trainer is the expected alternate. Representative trainers should define this rule before the interaction is treated as instruction.

cause: single hardcoded recovery path with no authorization context or alternative decision tree
Medium 3D route lacks lower-dimensional spatial equivalent

Semantic controls preserve selection, planning, failure, and recovery if Canvas is unavailable, but they reduce the route to names and totals. A simple ordered turn list or schematic is needed for people who cannot use the rendered depth view while preserving the same training outcome.

cause: no ordered waypoint list or spatial schematic in the telemetry panel
Direction / proposed corrections

Preserve the spatial world; repair the task model

The central ambition — the warehouse itself as the teaching surface — is sound. The corrections below protect the expressive 3D world while making its interaction evidence more trustworthy.

Direction to preserve

The warehouse reads as one authored training world. Orbit changes occlusion; route and anchors occupy the same world; obstruction and detour alter the spatial representation. Do not replace it with a flat dashboard or treat the 3D as an ornamental header. The next iteration should deepen that move with world-coordinate recovery and calibrated scale.

Proposed corrections

  1. World-coordinate recovery anchorWhen failure is injected, record the vehicle's current world position (x, z), not the route percentage. Build the detour route from that point to B-04. Recompute distance and progress relative to the new route geometry. This preserves the expressive camera and world while repairing the task model.
  2. Scale calibration bridgeAdd a coordinate-to-meters mapping derived from the floor grid (1 grid unit = 1 meter). Compute route distances from world coordinates and display them alongside the scale bar. This gives the 28 m / 36 m figures a defensible derivation without changing the visual grammar.
  3. Named camera bookmarksAdd three task-named views: "Origin W-17," "Blind corner approach C," and "Destination B-04." Each stores a yaw/pitch/zoom triplet. The trainee can compare the survey model to constrained travel perspectives without losing free orbit.
  4. Ordered waypoint listBelow the route metrics, show an ordered list of marked turns and segments (e.g., "W-17 → south 8m → turn east → crossing C → turn north → B-04"). This provides a lower-dimensional spatial equivalent for users who cannot use the rendered depth view.
  5. Trainer-authored recovery contextChange the recovery button label to include authorization: "Floor trainer approved: reroute via Aisle 6" or expose a small decision tree ("Stop and wait" / "Reroute" / "Call supervisor"). This distinguishes the fixture's routing suggestion from workplace safety protocol.

Review verdict: The 3D world is load-bearing for route rehearsal, not a decorative viewport. Its spatial relationships create coherent, product-specific training value. Repair the world-coordinate recovery anchor and scale calibration to make the interaction evidence trustworthy. Preserve the expressive camera, the persistent spatial relationships, and the industrial inspection grammar.

Scope and limits

This review used one local Chrome runtime, scripted state transitions, static source inspection, and two viewport renderings. It did not include representative warehouse personnel, actual equipment, calibrated spatial data, assistive-technology sessions, cross-browser testing, performance profiling, or professional accessibility, ergonomic, occupational-safety, or training-transfer assessment. Those omissions keep all human-factors and safety conclusions provisional.
