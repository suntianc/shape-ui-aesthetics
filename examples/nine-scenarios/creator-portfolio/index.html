Tidal Grammar

2026
Interactive installation

Six estuaries interrupt one another through light, live water data, and hand-built hydrophones.

Open project field note

Material score6 hydrophones
24-channel light
Browser instrument
9 × 14 m room

The water is never used as ambience. It conducts the room.

Recordings from brackish thresholds are analyzed locally into pressure and repetition. Visitors place a hand over one of six low tables; that gesture opens its tide into the shared light score while quieting the others. No visit produces the same hierarchy twice.

My role: concept, field recording, interaction score, visual system, and fabrication direction. Built with two acoustic engineers and a six-person installation crew.

02 / 06

The Weight of an Echo

2025
Sound sculpture

Porcelain vessels translate distant maintenance sounds into small, physical tremors.

Open project field note

Material score11 porcelain bodies
Contact transducers
18-minute loop
Bench-height installation

An echo gains weight when the listener must lean toward it.

Each vessel carries one infrastructural recording: a bridge joint cooling, a lift cable settling, an air vent closing after midnight. The low frequencies make glaze and table vibrate before the sound becomes recognizable.

My role: ceramic forms, recordings, score, and installation. The transducer mounts were developed with a mechanical fabricator so the porcelain remains replaceable and repairable.

03 / 06

Borrowed Weather

2025
Kinetic public work

A long textile instrument turns wind between two buildings into a score shared by dancers and passersby.

Open project field note

Material score42 m ripstop ribbon
Wind sensors
4 performers
Public passage

The choreography waits for a force nobody commissioned.

Four performers hold, release, and reroute a continuous ribbon according to gust strength. Small e-ink markers at street level translate the same measurements into three verbs—hold, cross, release—so a passerby can read the work without joining it.

My role: concept, spatial score, textile tests, and public interaction. The performance has a windless version using walking pace rather than simulated weather.

04 / 06

Soft Relay

2024
Participatory browser work

Seventy-three voices pass one unfinished sentence across eleven time zones without a central recording.

Open project field note

Material score73 contributors
11 time zones
Local audio fragments
Open browser edition

The network behaves less like an archive than a breath handed forward.

A visitor hears only the final four seconds submitted before them, then records a continuation. Their fragment is stored on-device until consent is confirmed. The public version exposes gaps and refusals as part of the score rather than smoothing them away.

My role: concept, interaction and consent model, interface, and editorial care. Code support by Ivo Chen; contributor names appear only where independently permitted.

05 / 06

Night Orchard

2024
Light and movement

Twenty-one hand-held lights reveal a choreography only through the places where bodies have just departed.

Open project field note

Material score21 portable lights
7 performers
27-minute score
Outdoor / indoor variants

The brightest event is an absence with a known shape.

Performers carry lights close to the ground, place them, and move on. Each lamp fades according to the duration of its last touch, leaving a legible history of decisions rather than a synchronized spectacle.

My role: light objects, temporal score, rehearsal direction, and access version. A seated arrangement preserves every spatial relationship without requiring audiences to move through darkness.

06 / 06

312 Small Suns

2023
Clay and stop-motion film

Three hundred and twelve thumb-sized clay discs record a year of studio heat, then become a six-minute film.

Open project field note

Material score312 clay discs
12 kiln firings
4,680 frames
6-minute silent film

A calendar can be held in one hand, but not all at once.

One disc was pressed at the end of each studio day. Its depth records the remaining warmth of the kiln shelf. In the film, discs appear by pressure rather than date, turning a private measure of work into a slow, uneven constellation.

My role: daily clay practice, camera system, edit, and exhibition arrangement. Missing days remain empty frames; the work does not invent continuity.

Biography

A practice of
listening back.

  • Born 1989 · tidal flats and repair shops
  • Based in Rotterdam · working internationally
  • Languages: English, Dutch, Portuguese
  • Available for commissions, exhibitions, and lectures

Mara Vale builds situations where matter can answer.

Her installations, performances, films, and browser works begin with close observation: pressure stored in clay, a building's night voice, weather moving between bodies, or the social life of an unfinished sentence.

Rather than represent those forces, she makes instruments that let them reorganize attention. Audiences are invited to listen, tune, carry, or interrupt—never merely to trigger an effect.

Vale's studio combines field recording, electronics, ceramic fabrication, interaction design, and long rehearsal. Each work includes a quieter parallel form for sites or bodies where motion, darkness, sound, bandwidth, or standing access is limited.

Current research follows repair as a temporal material: the trace left when an object returns to use but not to innocence. A new series for cracked loudspeakers and glazed seams is in development through 2026.
