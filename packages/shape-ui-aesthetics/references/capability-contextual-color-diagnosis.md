# Contextual Color Diagnosis

**Stable ID:** `contextual-color-diagnosis`  
**Class:** Core  
**Primary domain:** Color, Light & Material

## Observable trigger

Route here when the same token or measured color appears to change, vibrate, disappear, muddy, advance, recede, warm, or cool across different grounds, edges, areas, materials, placements, or displays.

## Non-trigger and near miss

Do not route here for a color-name disagreement, fixed legal brand matching, value-gamut compression, or a known color-management defect. If the whole palette lacks hierarchy rather than one relation behaving unexpectedly, return `reframe` and signal Color, Light & Material to the Director.

## Decision question

Which contextual variable causes the perceptual color event, and what is the smallest relational intervention that corrects it without destabilizing the whole system?

## Interpretation method

Separate factual sample from perceptual action. Hold the color constant while testing neighbors, ground, area, recurrence, boundary, light intensity, placement, material, display, and reading direction. Determine whether induction, simultaneous contrast, weak separation, overcontrast, edge relief, or reproduction is responsible. Correct the relation before editing the isolated token.

## Executable design procedure

1. Record measured/token values, output conditions, and the exact perceptual complaint.
2. List contextual variables and identify the smallest controlled comparison.
3. Test one variable at a time using duplicate samples across alternative grounds, areas, edges, or placements.
4. Classify the effect: connection, separation, vibration, disappearance, temperature shift, depth shift, or contamination.
5. Adjust ground, boundary, quantity, adjacency, placement, or local value before replacing the color.
6. Recheck the whole palette, semantics, contrast, target displays, and real content.

## Boundaries and failure modes

- Do not diagnose from screenshots or reproductions whose color pipeline is unknown.
- Do not treat numeric hue, value, or purity as a perceptual formula.
- Preserve fixed semantic and brand requirements; alter permitted relations around them.
- Avoid strengthening every edge until the system becomes aggressive and brittle.
- Escalate calibrated scientific color requirements to current colorimetry and device measurement.

## Attached Reference Knowledge

Induction lets neighboring colors influence one another; contrast produces separation and force. Too little separation muddies, while universal hard contrast destroys relational subtlety. Color memory and color names are weak evidence compared with controlled side-by-side observation.

## Runtime Self-Critique

Ask whether the cause was isolated rather than guessed, whether the fix changes fewer relationships than replacing the token, and whether it survives real output conditions and required semantics.
