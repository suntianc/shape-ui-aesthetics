# Light, Form & Shadow

**Stable ID:** `light-form-and-shadow`  
**Class:** Core  
**Primary domain:** Color, Light & Material

## Observable trigger

Route here when light and shadow must reveal form, hierarchy, depth, state, or atmosphere, but current illumination is decorative, uniformly bright, mechanically symmetrical, or too dark to preserve consequential action.

## Non-trigger and near miss

Do not route here when failure is caused by bad geometry, normals, roughness, exposure, display transform, or missing environment. A deliberately flat, silhouette, or unlit visual language is not defective when it serves the thesis and task.

## Decision question

Which light and shadow responsibilities make form, priority, material, and state legible from the delivery viewpoint without exposing an arbitrary lighting recipe?

## Interpretation method

Translate reference light into observable softness, intensity relation, color relation, spatial throw, and temporal behavior. Then assign responsibilities: dominant shaping and direction, continuation of gradients and dark information, and separation only where needed. Judge total contribution at the subject, not lamp count or panel values. Let shadow color arise from illuminant, reflection, environment, material, and induction rather than adding black to local color.

## Executable design procedure

1. Lock camera, subject movement, environment, exposure, materials, narrative, and required states.
2. Record softness, relative intensity, color, throw, and animation evidence for each motivated source.
3. Build a form read showing which planes, edges, and actions must separate.
4. Establish one dominant shaping relation, then add only the continuation and separation needed.
5. Audit shadow information and color as part of the same object and environment.
6. Remove unmotivated rims, symmetric fill, ambient wash, and atmosphere that hides action.
7. Validate extreme poses, backgrounds, materials, time, reduced effects, and target outputs.

## Boundaries and failure modes

- Do not copy source names, lamp counts, or obsolete numeric presets instead of visible evidence.
- Do not use fill to bury a weak key or global underexposure to simulate darkness.
- Do not add backlight as a mandatory three-point formula.
- Preserve important actions and states in dark scenes.
- Avoid culturally biased beauty conventions and verify current HDR, gamut, and exposure pipelines.

## Attached Reference Knowledge

Softness, intensity, color, throw, and animation interact; they are not independent sliders. Lighting roles describe responsibilities, not fixtures. Convincing shadow belongs to the object's color world while still allowing selective abstraction when that is the explicit language.

Ask whether form reads without labels, whether each light has a responsibility and motivation, whether darkness preserves action, and whether a pipeline defect was mistakenly styled around.
