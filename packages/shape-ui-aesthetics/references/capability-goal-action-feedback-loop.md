# Goal–Action–Feedback Loop

**Stable ID:** `goal-action-feedback-loop`  
**Class:** Core  
**Primary domain:** Interaction, Agency & Wayfinding

## Observable trigger

Route here when the task ontology and action vocabulary are already accepted but users lose the connection at a specific hop between intent, available action, system state change, feedback, evaluation, and completion.

## Non-trigger and near miss

Do not route here when objects, actions, properties, relationships, or terms are themselves wrong; return `reframe` and signal Interaction, Agency & Wayfinding for conceptual-model work. Do not route here when the function is missing, information is merely dense, or a known infrastructure defect solely causes delay.

## Decision question

Where does the loop from goal through execution to evaluation diverge, and which scent, mapping, state, feedback, or recovery repair closes it?

## Interpretation method

Trace the loop from user goal and intention through chosen action, physical input, mapping, application state, rendered output, perception, interpretation, and next decision. Record latency, direction, gain, degrees of freedom, noise, occlusion, prediction, and recovery where relevant. At every choice point, provide information scent in the user's goal language. Place feedback near the action or next scan path and scale interruption to consequence.

## Executable design procedure

1. State the user's goal and expected completion in their terms.
2. Diagram every hop from intention to perceived result.
3. Mark the first divergence between user and system models.
4. Run a minimal controlled comparison to isolate scent, control, mapping, state, latency, placement, or interpretation.
5. Repair the earliest responsible hop and keep feedback local and proportional.
6. Make completion, unsaved work, undo, retrieval, and other closing steps explicit.
7. Rewalk the entire loop under error, interruption, repeated input, assistive input, and real latency.

## Boundaries and failure modes

- Do not blame gestures or users before isolating system mapping and feedback.
- Do not use strong interruption for minor feedback or rely on color alone.
- Do not treat a closed loop as proof that the task itself is valuable or safe.
- Escalate motion sickness, occupational health, collision, and disability participation to professional human-factors review.
- Revalidate device latency and tracking rather than inheriting historical thresholds.

## Attached Reference Knowledge

Users follow goal scent, act, and evaluate whether they moved closer. Peripheral vision notices motion but cannot read detailed feedback; consequential messages belong near attention. Spatial systems add sensor, mapping, and embodiment hops where visual and proprioceptive conflict can break the loop.

## Runtime Self-Critique

Ask whether users can predict the action, perceive the result where they are looking, evaluate progress, and recover; verify that added feedback treats the cause rather than decorating the failure.
