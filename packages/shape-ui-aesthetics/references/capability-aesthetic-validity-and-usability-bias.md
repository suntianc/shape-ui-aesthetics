# Aesthetic Validity & Usability Bias

**Stable ID:** `aesthetic-validity-and-usability-bias`  
**Class:** Core  
**Primary domain:** Coherence, Critique & Evidence

## Observable trigger

Route here when visual appeal, polish, familiarity, or positive emotion may be mistaken for task success, trustworthiness, or absence of behavioral failure.

## Non-trigger and near miss

Do not route here merely to emphasize a CTA or to dismiss aesthetics as superficial. If behavior has already been independently proven and the open question is aesthetic fit, preserve that authority separation.

## Decision question

Which judgments reflect perceived usability created by appearance, which reflect observed behavior, and how can the work retain valid aesthetic value while repairing real failure?

## Interpretation method

Separate aesthetic response, predicted ease, observed task behavior, and technical integrity. Treat positive affect as a real product effect that may increase exploration and tolerance, while checking whether it masks errors, delay, misunderstanding, or abandonment. Compare what users say with what they accomplish and recover from under representative conditions.

## Executable design procedure

1. State the aesthetic claim and the behavioral claim separately.
2. Collect appearance-blind task evidence where practical: completion, error, time, comprehension, confidence, and recovery.
3. Identify where polish, familiarity, or brand prestige biases interpretation.
4. Preserve aesthetic relationships that support engagement or comprehension.
5. Repair the observed behavioral failure without using ugliness as proof of seriousness.
6. Re-test task behavior and ask for human aesthetic judgment as separate verdicts.

## Boundaries and failure modes

- Do not treat a beautiful artifact as usable without behavioral evidence.
- Do not treat usability evidence as authority over identity, cultural fit, or beauty.
- Do not generalize one culture's or industry's prestige signals.
- Avoid turning this audit into anti-aesthetic minimalism.
- Keep technical blockers and aesthetic rejection independently blocking.

## Attached Reference Knowledge

Aesthetic quality changes perceived usability and willingness to explore; that effect is meaningful but can bias research. Observable behavior and human aesthetic acceptance answer different questions and cannot be averaged into one score.

## Runtime Self-Critique

Ask whether praise corresponds to successful behavior, whether behavioral rigor has flattened the work, and whether each conclusion names the evidence and authority it actually has.
