# Aesthetic Thesis & Artistic Conception

**Stable ID:** `aesthetic-thesis-and-artistic-conception`  
**Class:** Core  
**Primary domain:** Meaning, Culture & Aesthetic Thesis

## Observable trigger

Route here when a direction has visual adjectives, references, or effects but no product-specific proposition connecting what the product means, what the user does, and what the interface makes perceptible.

## Non-trigger and near miss

Do not route here merely because a thesis needs clearer wording. If the protagonist, ritual, generative relationships, and intended afterimage already govern the artifact, return `reframe` and signal the relevant material domain to the Director. A mood-board request with no product or task evidence is an incomplete assignment frame, not yet a thesis problem.

## Decision question

What experiential proposition makes this product's meaning inseparable from its protagonist, ritual, perceptual world, and formal behavior?

## Interpretation method

Observe the product across detail, whole, change, and relationship. Separate visible evidence from generic category conventions. Name the core tension, then choose a concrete protagonist able to embody it. Treat emotion and environment as reciprocal: let the intended feeling determine selection, scale, rhythm, light, sound, and omission; let those choices make the feeling experientially available.

Test every image or metaphor for necessity. If a different image could replace it without changing the experience, it is decorative. Extend the thesis beyond literal statement through implication, absence, threshold, or continuation, but never confuse blur, vagueness, or empty space with artistic conception by itself.

## Executable design procedure

1. Record product truth, user ritual, and four observations: local detail, whole system, change over time, and consequential relationships.
2. Name one central tension and three visible or behavioral properties that express it.
3. Propose a concrete protagonist and test two substitutes; keep it only if substitution weakens the meaning or ritual.
4. State the desired afterimage as a felt and remembered consequence, not a visual style.
5. Write one thesis connecting protagonist, ritual, tension, afterimage, and a generative formal relationship.
6. Derive a small set of consequences for composition, medium, color/material, motion, interaction, and information behavior without choosing arbitrary components.
7. Define one Signature Move that makes the thesis perceptible through use.
8. Delete any direct explanation, effect, or motif that repeats the thesis without deepening the experience.

## Boundaries and failure modes

- Do not use atmosphere, filtering, ambiguity, or whitespace as proof of meaning.
- Do not sacrifice factual clarity, task completion, accessibility, or cultural permission for emotional effect.
- Do not surface-impose a historical or community-specific aesthetic; require accountable evidence and human judgment when such meaning is consequential.
- Do not let a spectacular protagonist become a decorative backdrop disconnected from the ritual.
- Reframe if product truth and user purpose are too weak to support a specific thesis.

## Attached Reference Knowledge

Proportion and formal rules can support coherence but do not define beauty. Preserve a generative constraint while allowing purposeful exception. Translate sensory qualities by relationships—pressure, rhythm, edge, interval, mass, temperature, pace—not by copying a source's surface. Transform form through the product's identity and use, not through novelty alone.

## Required Capability Contribution

Return exactly the canonical seven fields from `routing-and-reconciliation.md`: `decision`, `evidence`, `recommendation`, `consequence`, `boundary`, `validation`, and `onward`. Do not add a mini-report.

Populate those fields with the decision key; evidence showing why the current direction is interchangeable; one protagonist-centered thesis; desired afterimage; the governing generative relationship and Signature Move; consequences for at least two other domains; boundaries and validation obligations; and `resolved`, `reframe`, `validate`, or `human-choice`.

## Runtime Self-Critique

Ask whether the thesis could describe an unrelated product, whether the protagonist is structural, whether the ritual changes the experience, and whether the afterimage is observable in the artifact. Flag any cultural or emotional claim that needs accountable human choice.
